Learnstream Beta

Note: Learnstream Beta is no longer maintained and not available on GitHub in accordance with health regulations.

Learnstream Beta was my first project with @npisenti, while we were undergrads at Harvey Mudd College. We were in love with the idea of spaced repetition, so we created a tool that lets you create, share and study cards for just about anything, with support for text documents and YouTube videos. Learnstream Atomic

Learnstream Atomic was created in the summer of 2011 for Harvey Mudd College with @npisenti, @cmarvin, and @Memento-Mori. Our target audience was students entering Harvey Mudd that wanted to brush up on physics and calculus.

Some concepts for physics

Atomic again used the concept of spaced repetition, but we had a more sophisticated user model that allowed solving a single problem to count as a review for several knowledge components (called "concepts" in the interface). We represented knowledge components as questions, which were answered in our concept dictionary. Users can scrutinize the system's evaluation of their strength for each component.

Physics lessons

The material for physics was presented as lessons using video from MIT OpenCourseWare and Khan Academy. The video lessons included embedded questions. After viewing a lesson, relevant concepts were unlocked so that they could be practiced and reviewed with the study interface.

Learnstream Rudinium

Rudinium was intended as a "video dictionary" for the material we filmed for Harvey Mudd's Real Analysis course. Users can search for a definition or theorem in the course and then see a description and video clip.

Entry for "bijection"

Of course, we didn't stop there. We created quizzes for many of the items so that they could all be studied on a spaced repetition schedule.
